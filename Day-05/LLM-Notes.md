# Learn About LLMs - Short Notes

## What is an LLM?

LLM (Large Language Model) is an AI model trained on a huge amount of text data. It can understand and generate human-like text, answer questions, write code, summarize content, and more.

**Example:** ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude.

---

## What is a Token?

A token is the basic unit of text processed by an LLM. A token can be a word, part of a word, punctuation mark, or symbol.

**Example:**

```text
"Hello World"
```

may be split into tokens like:

```text
Hello | World
```

---

## What is Context Window?

A context window is the amount of information an LLM can remember and process in a single conversation.

A larger context window allows the model to handle longer conversations and larger documents without forgetting earlier information.

---

## What is Temperature?

Temperature controls the randomness and creativity of an AI model's responses.

* Low Temperature (0.1 - 0.3): More accurate and predictable answers.
* Medium Temperature (0.5 - 0.7): Balanced responses.
* High Temperature (0.8 - 1.0): More creative and varied responses.

---

## What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt Engineering is the process of designing clear and effective prompts to get better results from AI models.

A well-written prompt usually produces more accurate and useful responses.

**Example:**

Poor Prompt:

```text
Tell me about Python.
```

Better Prompt:

```text
Explain Python programming for beginners with examples.
```

---

## Conclusion

LLMs are powerful AI systems that process text using tokens. Their performance depends on factors such as context window, temperature, and the quality of prompts provided by the user.
